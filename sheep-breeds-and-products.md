# Sheep Breeds and Products

**Summary**: Source-specific account of sheep breeds grouped by milk, wool, meat, and mixed production, with emphasis on Italian breeds and product uses.

**Sources**: raw/Zootechnics.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14

---

The source organizes sheep breeds by production purpose and repeatedly links morphology to milk, meat, wool, and local cheese production. It treats breed choice as a practical decision shaped by region, rearing system, twinning rate, lamb growth, fleece traits, and cheese yield. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 28-33, 37)

## Dairy Breeds

The Delle Langhe breed is described as a sheep of the Langhe region that produces good quantities of milk and meat. The source gives 130 to 180 liters of milk over a 6 to 7 month lactation, females of about 40 to 50 kg, males of 60 to 65 kg, no horns in either sex, open white fleece, and conversion of milk into Robiola delle Langhe cheese. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 28)

The Sarda breed is described as originally Sardinian and now present in several Italian regions and Mediterranean countries. The source presents it as a dairy breed with thin strong legs, white open fleece, good meat from suckling lambs of 8 to 10 kg at 30 days, and twinning that is low under free grazing but higher under intensive conditions. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 28)

The Comisana, also called "redhead," is described as a Sicilian-related dairy breed with reddish honey-colored head and cheeks, white fleece with red or brown markings, about 140 kg of milk over 220 days, suckling lambs of 8 to 8.5 kg at 30 days, and a twinning rate of 60 to 70 percent. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 29)

The Sicilian or Pinzirita breed is described as produced by crossing an indigenous breed with Barbareschi, Comisani, and Sardi rams. The source treats it as dual-purpose but mainly milk-oriented, with 90 kg of milk over 240 days, a 20 percent cheese yield, and good twinning. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 29)

The Barbaresca is described as produced by crossing Tunisian rams with Sicilian ewes and as one of the source's best triple-purpose breeds because it balances milk, meat, and wool. The source emphasizes large size, semi-open white fleece, a fatty deposit at the base of the tail, 110 to 120 kg of milk over 210 days, and rapid lamb weight gain. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 30)

The Moscia Leccese is described as a Puglia breed adapted to Salento and associated with semi-wild flocks of 50 to 100 animals. The source describes white or sometimes black fleece, coarse wool for mattresses, black hair on the face, ears, and legs, milk plus meat purpose, and low twinning of 25 percent. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 30)

The Valle del Belice is described as a western Sicilian dairy breed used in wild and semi-wild systems. The source gives male weights of 90 to 100 kg, female weights of 60 to 70 kg, white fleece, red markings around the eyes or ears, lamb weights of 8 to 10 kg at 30 days, and a twinning rate of about 45 percent. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 30)

## Wool and Triple-Purpose Breeds

The Merino is described as a wool-producing breed group whose fine wool is suited to textiles and knitwear. The source says the Australian Merino is considered the king of merino breeds because of wool quality and quantity, and it describes tight fleece with fine, dense, elastic, long strands. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 31)

The Gentile di Puglia is described as the best Italian breed for textile wool because of improvement through Merino crosses. The source gives white fine wool, raw wool production of about 6 kg in rams and 3.5 kg in ewes, milk production of 80 to 100 liters, and milk fat of 8 to 11 percent. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 32)

The Sopravissana is listed under triple-purpose breeds but the source says its wool quality and quantity make it especially notable for wool production. The source describes origin from crossing Apennine transhumant sheep with Merino rams, meat from lambs of 10 to 12 kg at 30 days, about 50 kg of milk net of lamb suckling, and annual wool production of about 6 to 7 kg in males and 3.5 to 4.5 kg in females. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 33)

## Products and Production Logic

The source treats sheep products as an integrated system. Milk is linked to cheese and income, lamb slaughter timing is linked to lactation economics, wool value is linked to fiber quality and market competition, and breed selection is linked to whether the flock is managed for milk, meat, wool, or mixed output. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 27, 33, 37)
