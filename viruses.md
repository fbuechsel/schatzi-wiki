# Viruses

**Summary**: Source summary of how viruses are described and why the text places them outside the kingdom scheme.

**Sources**: raw/Zoology.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14

---

The source describes viruses as particles made from nucleic acid and a protein capsid. It says they are small enough to pass through porcelain filters and treats that physical smallness as one of the reasons they historically stood out from ordinary cellular organisms. (source: Zoology.pdf - Page 14)

## Why the source places viruses outside the kingdoms

This discussion appears directly alongside the source's five-kingdom overview. Viruses are said not to fit within ordinary kingdom classification because they are not true cells and because they carry out vital functions only when using the metabolism of host organisms. (source: Zoology.pdf - Page 14)

That position makes [viruses](viruses.md) an exception inside the source's general map of life. The kingdoms are built around cellular organisms, while viruses are presented as biologically active but structurally simpler and dependent in a way that resists the same classificatory scheme. (source: Zoology.pdf - Page 14)

## Disease examples and caution

The source describes viruses as parasites able to attack bacteria and eukaryotic cells and then lists several diseases associated with them. Because that disease list includes cholera, this page should be used carefully whenever modern medical accuracy matters. (source: Zoology.pdf - Page 14)

This is therefore one of the clearest places where the wiki should preserve what the source says while redirecting readers to current public-health references. The goal is to keep provenance without treating the source's disease list as a modern authority. (source: Zoology.pdf - Page 14)
