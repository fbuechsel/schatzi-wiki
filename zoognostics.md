# Zoognostics

**Summary**: Source-specific account of cattle zoognostics as external body-region assessment used in livestock description and evaluation.

**Sources**: raw/Zootechnics.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14

---

The source uses cattle zoognostics for the practical description of an animal's external body regions. It divides the body into five major regions: head, neck, torso, front legs, and rear legs. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 20)

## Body Regions

The source subdivides the major body regions into zoognostic regions such as sinciput, forehead, horn, nose, nostrils, muzzle, lips, chin, eyes, ears, cheek, temple, neck, dewlap, withers, back, loins, rump, chest, brisket, side, sternum, abdomen, breast, tail, perineum, shoulder, arm, elbow, forearm, knee, shin, fetlock, pastern, corona, foot, thigh, buttock, stifle, leg, and hock. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 20)

## Relation to Anatomy

The source notes that zoognostic regions do not always perfectly match anatomical regions. It still links some terms to anatomical structures: withers to the spinous apophyses of the first 5 to 6 dorsal vertebrae, shin to metacarpals and metatarsals, fetlock to phalangeal metacarpal and metatarsal joints, pastern to the first two phalanges, stifle to femur-tibia-patella joints, and hock to the lower tibia epiphysis and tarsal joint. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 20)

## Role in Breed Description

Zoognostics matters in the source because breed cards repeatedly describe stature, withers, rump, thighs, limbs, udders, coat, and other visible features. It gives a shared vocabulary for connecting [[cattle-breeds]] to [[cattle-farming]] and production assessment. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 5-20)
