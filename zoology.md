# Zoology

**Summary**: Summary page for the source document *Compendium of Zoology*. It surveys classification, major organism groups, animal phyla, evolution, human origins, and Mendelian genetics from an introductory biology perspective.

**Sources**: raw/Zoology.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14

---

This source presents zoology as part of a larger introductory biology framework rather than as a narrow survey of animals alone. Its opening pages connect the subject to Aristotle's early classification work, Darwin's evolutionary theory, Mendel's laws of heredity, and Lorenz's work on animal behavior. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

## Scope of the source

The book is organized into five main areas: [[biological-classification]], the five-kingdom model of life, the structure and diversity of [[animals]], [[biological-evolution]], and [[mendelian-genetics]]. That means the ingest touches both zoological topics and broader life-science concepts that the source treats as necessary background for understanding animal life. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

The classification chapters explain taxonomy, binomial nomenclature, Linnaean ranks, and the shift from fixist classification toward evolutionary interpretation. The organism chapters then move through [[monera]], [[protista]], [[fungi]], plants, and animals, using the source's five-kingdom framework as the main map of life. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

## Animal-side content

The animal chapters focus on defining animal traits, embryonic development, and the major phyla. The source treats porifera, coelenterates, platyhelminthes, annelids, molluscs, echinoderms, arthropods, and chordates as the main steps in its overview of body-plan complexity and evolutionary diversification. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

Within [[vertebrates]], the source moves from fish to amphibians, reptiles, birds, and mammals, then places humans inside mammals and primates before shifting into a simplified narrative of [[human-evolution]]. This creates a bridge between descriptive zoology and the source's evolutionary storyline. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

## Evolution and heredity

The later chapters explain the contrast between fixism, Lamarck's transformism, and Darwinian natural selection. They also tie fossils, branching evolution, and common ancestry into the classification scheme introduced earlier in the book. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

The closing genetics material frames heredity as a second major foundation for biological explanation. Mendel's pea-plant experiments, genotype and phenotype, dominance, segregation, independent assortment, multiple alleles, and polygenic inheritance are all used to explain how variability arises and is transmitted. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)

## Notes on use

This wiki should treat the source as an introductory biology compendium with a clear historical and textbook flavor. Some parts of the source use dated or superseded scientific framing, especially around [[five-kingdoms-of-nature]], [[viruses]], [[human-evolution]], and parts of [[mendelian-genetics]], so those pages include further-reading links for current context. (source: Zoology.pdf - Pages 6-64)
