# Zootechnics

**Summary**: Summary page for *Course on Veterinary Techniques - Zootechnics*. The source surveys practical livestock husbandry for cattle, sheep, goats, equids, and pigs, with emphasis on breeds, production uses, breeding systems, reproduction, pregnancy, birth, and lactation.

**Sources**: raw/Zootechnics.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-05-14

---

*Zootechnics* is an applied veterinary and livestock-production source rather than a general zoology text. Its table of contents divides the course into cattle farming, sheep farming, horse farming, and pig farming, while goat rearing appears inside the sheep chapter and donkeys, mules, and hinnies appear inside the horse chapter. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Page 2)

## Scope of the Source

The source treats livestock through production purpose, breed morphology, management system, and reproductive technique. Cattle are divided mainly into dairy, meat, and dual-purpose categories, sheep are organized around milk, wool, meat, and triple-purpose production, horses are presented through equine ethnography and selected breeds, and pigs are discussed through industrial production, breed type, and reproduction. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 3, 27, 39, 51)

The document is strongly centered on Italian husbandry. It discusses Italian cattle breeds such as Chianina, Marchigiana, Piedmontese, Modican, and Italian Friesian, Italian sheep breeds such as Sarda, Comisana, Gentile di Puglia, and Sopravissana, Italian goat breed groupings, Italian donkey breeds, and Italian pig breeds such as Cinta Senese and Casertana. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 5, 10, 16-18, 28-33, 38, 47-48, 54-55)

## Main Husbandry Areas

The cattle chapter is the largest practical framework in the source. It covers milk composition, dairy breeds, meat breeds, cattle age and production categories, zoognostic body regions, rearing systems, reproduction, artificial insemination, embryo transfer, pregnancy, birth, postpartum care, and milk production. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 3-26)

The sheep and goat material combines breed cataloguing with production systems. It explains why Italian sheep husbandry emphasizes dairy sheep and sheep cheese, why wool production has declined, how sheep breeds are grouped by milk, wool, meat, or combined purposes, and how goat rearing is mainly oriented toward milk in the Western world. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 27-38)

The equid material includes horses, donkeys, mules, and hinnies. It classifies horses and donkeys within Equidae, describes selected horse breeds and their uses, gives reproduction and birth guidance for mares, and treats mules and hinnies as hybrids with practical value for carrying and pulling. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 39-50)

The pig chapter focuses on industrial pig farming in Italy. It describes heavy-pig production, free-range, outdoor-bred, and indoor systems, breed types, Italian and foreign breeds, and reproduction management. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 51-59)

## Limitations and Source-Specific Context

The PDF was generated from a 2015 Word document and contains translated instructional prose, so some terms and phrases are awkward or internally inconsistent. The sheep chapter, for example, describes the ewe oestrous cycle using phase labels that appear garbled in translation, and the horse chapter has the heading "REPRODUCTIONE TECHNIQUES." (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 35, 45)

Several management and surgical practices are preserved here as source-specific descriptions, not as modern welfare guidance. [livestock-welfare-and-regulation](livestock-welfare-and-regulation.md) records that caution for procedures such as castration, tail docking, dehorning, and manual removal of retained placenta, all of which the source discusses in practical husbandry terms and sometimes says require veterinary involvement. (source: Zootechnics.pdf - Pages 25, 36)
